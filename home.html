My name is Martin Jansen, after completing my masters in communication science (commerce and communication) at Radboud University (Nijmegen, NL) I had a feeling that I was not done yet, with regard to science. 

During my studies I developed a particular interest in consumer behaviour, innovations and the continuous interactions between senders (or organisations) and receivers (or users/customers). This led me to search for opportunities to advance my understand if these topics, which I found in the form of a PhD position at the University of Duisburg-Essen in Germany at the chair for Social Psychology: Media and Communication led by Prof. Dr. Kr√§mer. 

In my current dissertation work I investigate the effects of Political Micro-targeting on social networks. I specifically look at using disclosures as a measure to increase transparency in order to inform users about the practices taking place on platforms that might be invisible to them. 

While conducting research on this, I try to continue to improve my statistical skillset as well as getting more familiar with transparency myself, in this case open science practices.
